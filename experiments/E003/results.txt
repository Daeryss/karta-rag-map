--- observation ---
3000/300 is the only configuration where all three authored queries return
their expected top-1 file. Q1 (direct factual) is robust across all four
configs but cosine score monotonically decreases with chunk size. Q2 (specific
detail) accuracy collapses at 6000/500 — relevant signal is diluted by larger
surrounding context. Q3 (cross-language conceptual, answer in Scala) misses at
both <3000 configs and hits at ≥3000; the smaller-chunk failures return Java
files (KafkaProducer.java, KafkaAdminClient.java) with cosine higher than the
correct Scala file's cosine at larger configs — i.e. absolute cosine does not
differentiate correctness, only within-config ranking does.

Total embed wall-clock is U-shaped in chunk size: minimum at 3000/300 (8374 ms),
1.38× speedup over 750/100 (11580 ms). Per-chunk cost rises roughly linearly with
chunk size (7→11→21→48 ms), but total chunk count falls faster — fixed per-chunk
overhead (HTTP round-trip, Lucene write) dominates at small chunks.

This experiment locks Karta defaults v0.1.0 chunking at 3000/300.

CAVEATS (per v0.2 §8 threats to validity):
  * n = 3 authored queries, single author — not a gold query set.
  * 10-file pilot slice, ~30K LOC, does not yet meet methodology §3.1
    minimum of ≥100k LOC. Generalisation to full Kafka untested.
  * Single embedding model (nomic-embed-text). U-shape may be model-specific.
  * "By-eye" correctness assessment, not Recall@k against gold annotation.
